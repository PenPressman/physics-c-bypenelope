Briefly, most of what you need to learn for this unit are relationships between variables, so I’ll just say this: there are too many “this is the derivative of this” and “this is the integral from this to this of this” to perfectly memorize at first (at least for me), so maybe spend extra time making sure you understand. Even if combined with memorizing, this will help you check that your formulas are logical as you write them. And, I find it helpful to check the units of what you are multiplying to confirm the units of your result. Oh, and sign convention gets weird because there are a lot of scalars here, but I’ll explain that for each concept below. Have fun!

Center of Mass

algebraic: COMx (or swap for y)= Σ m1r1+m2r2)/(m1+m2)

*remember that “m1” is just an example, add as many as you have/need.

calulus: Xcom (or y/z)=1/Mtotal ∫ xdm

Energy

kinetic energy: K=1/2mv2

gravitational potential energy: Ug=mgh(relative height)

potential energy from a spring: Us=1/2kx2

”loseable energy”: Q=µkFNdisplacement

elastic collisions: v2i-v1i = -(v2f-v1f) *aka, “relative approach” equation

conservation of energy: E total before = E total after


Work and Power

work: W=F•d (functionally, force times the parallel component of displacement)

ΣW=ΣF•D

ΣW = ∆K = 1/2m(vf2+vi2)

power: P=dw/dt, if constant: P=W/t

P=F•v

average power: Pavg=W/t

work concerning non-constant forces: W= ∫ F•dror x (where the limits of integration are either xi and xf or ri and rf

Mometum and Impulse

momentum: p=mv

impulse: J=∆p

impulse when force is constant: J = F∆T

impulse when force is with respect to time: J = ∫ F(t)dt from ti to tf

impulse with an average force value J = Favg∆T

impulse when given Ft graph: J = AUC

conservation of momentum: P total before = P total after

SI Units for Energy and Momentum

Quick note (which you’ll see on every topic page), as long as your units are consistent when you are converting things, use whatever version you want, just remember what the base units are.

Also, I’m considering each set of units cumulative, so make sure you know any units from topics before this (i.e. kinematics, Newton’s laws).

energy joules [J}
work joules [J] or newton meters [Nm]
power watts [W]
momentum kilogram meter per second [kgm/s 2]
impulse newton second [Ns}
