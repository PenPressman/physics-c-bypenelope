You’ve probably heard bits about relativity given its fame and propensity for use in catch phrases and puns. Basically, until Einstein started figuring it all out, there were two separate components of our understanding of the universe. One, thanks to Renee Descartes, was the Cartesian plane: aka a 3D plane structured through x, y, and z coordinates. This description gave us our understanding of movement and location in space. Newton helped bring time into the equation (metaphorically speaking, given that the whole point is space and time were not actually in the same equation yet). He built our understanding of time, motion, and the forces governing the universe. Finally, Einstein realized that space and time could not be held separate. He discovered that the universe is really a manifold (bendable layered plane, basically), in which space and time, now “space-time,” could be curved by energy and matter. In a nutshell: special relativity explains how things are affected by space-time, while general relativity explains why space-time is malleable in the first place, and how it moves.
