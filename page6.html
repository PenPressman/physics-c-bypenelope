Welcome to the start of the next “class” of AP Physics C. Electrostatics and Magnetism! Starting now, every new concept is technically separate from all previous units. HOWEVER, since motion and forces come up again, things like kinematics and first principles will be helpful in solving new problems. You’ll see how circular motion eventually comes up too. I’ve tried to format this digital resource in a way that allows you to just read through things (no diagrams, just explanations), because for me, forcing myself to picture things and draw everything myself is helpful. For this unit, though, there will be some things that are difficult to just think through/picture, so I’ll make note of when you really should draw things out. Of course, you can also just use this as a “read though before your test” type of thing. This unit will focus on particles, their charges, and the forces that charge creates. And, how that all influences motion and a bunch of other stuff… specifics to come!

SI Units and Constants for Electrostatics

Units

charge (q/Q) coulombs [C]
electric potential (V) volts, or joules per coulomb: [V] or [J/C]
electric potential energy (UE joules [J]
electric field (E) newtons per coulomb [N/C]
electric flux (symbol here) newton meters squared per coulomb [Nm2/C]

Constants

charge of an electron (qe) -1.6E-19C
mass of an electron (me) 9.11E-31kg
charge of a proton (qp) 1.6E-19C
mass of a proton (mp) 1.673E-27kg
charge of a neutron (qn) 0C
mass of a neutron (mn) 1.675E-27kg
permittivity of free space (ε0) 8.85E-12 C2/Nm2
electrostatic constant (K) 1/4π ε0, 9E9 Nm2/C2 (*for point charges)
