Circuits










Formatted Paragraph

The good news about this unit is there is very little to memorize. The bad news is, problems can get very confusing very quickly, so it's extra important to keep track of everything. When in doubt, write down all the things you know must be true, and draw the clearest possible versions of every problem. It's hard to explain this before we get to the details, but remember: combine what you can, draw every loop, and don’t mix up series vs parallel calculations.

Basic Circuits

V=IR

Power Equations

P=VI (*I remember this as VI=P, as in “VIP”)
P=V2/R
P=I2R

total E dissipated: E=Pt

Equivalent Resistance

in series: R12 = Req in parallel: 1/R12 = Req

**Note, equivalent resistance is sometimes denoted as “RT”

Capacitor Circuits

potential difference: ∆V=Ed **review E-fields!
charge in a capacitor: Q=CV

dielectrics: C=K ε0 A/d (where “A” is the area of the plates) (where “K” is the dielectric constant)

parallel plate capacitors: C = ε0 A/d (where “A” is the area of the plates)
cyllyndrial capacitors: C = 2π ε0/L ln(b/a)
spherical capacitors: C = 4π ε0(ab/(b-a))

Equivalent Capacitance

In series:1/C12 = Ceq In parallel: C12 = Ceq

Energy

in a capacitor: Ucap=1/2CV2
in a capacitor: Ucap=Q22C

energy density: μ= 1/2ε0E2

Resistance Capacitor Circuits

“tau,” aka RC circuit time constant: τ=RC =63.2% of total voltage

charge over time: q(t)= Q(1-e-t/RC)
voltage in a capacitor over time: Vcap(t)= ε(1-e-t/RC)
voltage in a resistor over time: VR(t)= εe-t/RC
current over time: i(t)= Imaxe-t/RC

Other

resistance of a wire: R= ρL/A (where “L” is the length of the wire and “A” is the area of the cross section)

resistivity: ρ=E/J
current density:J=i/A
conductivity: σ=1/ρ

voltage of a battery: V = ε-1Rinternal (where “ε” is EMF, aka terminal voltage, aka electromotive force)

Intro

SI Units for Circuits


current (I) amps [a]
voltage (v) volts [v] (volts = joules per coulomb, j/c)
resistance (R) ohms [Ω]
capacitance (C) farads [f] (farrads = coulombs per volt, c/v)
energy density (μ) joule per cubic meter [J/m2]
resistivity (ρ) ohm meters [Ωm]
tau (τ) seconds [s]
